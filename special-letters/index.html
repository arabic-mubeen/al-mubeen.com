# Rule of Stopping

It is more natural in speech to dismiss ending vowels when stopping at a word (e.g: taking a breath, full stop, etc.). We may disregard this rule in lessons to clarify correct pronunciation.

The rule of stopping applies only to short vowels (Tashkeel) and Tanween; Long vowels (Alif, Waw, Yaa) will still be pronounced. Only exception is Tanweet FatH (Ø§Ù‹), which is usually pronounced despite stopping.

# Taa Marbuta (Ø©)

Taa Marbuta (meaning bound Taa) is just Taa (Øª) and Haa (Ø©) joined together.
